Origins and Context

Kenya has established itself as one of Africa's most dynamic technology hubs, often described as the "Silicon Savannah." Within this environment, opportunity and constraint coexist: access to global tools and open-source ecosystems contrasts sharply with limitations in capital, infrastructure, and institutional support. It is within this context that my professional identity has taken shape.

Professional Focus: Software Development as Infrastructure

At the core of my professional profile is software development—not as an end in itself, but as infrastructure for business and automation. The emphasis is on building systems that:

  • Are modular and extensible
  • Can operate in low-cost or local environments
  • Integrate cleanly with third-party tools when advantageous
  • Remain portable across hosting models (static, serverless, cloud, hybrid)

Omix Systems: Platform Vision

At the center of this trajectory is Omix Systems, an ambitious venture conceived not as a single product company, but as a modular technology platform capable of expanding into an integrated ecosystem of software, infrastructure, and enterprise services.

Long-Term Vision

The forward-looking ambition is explicit: to build Omix Systems into a durable, multi-layered technology organization with compounding leverage. This includes a portfolio of interoperable products, internal tooling that accelerates new venture creation, automation-first operations that reduce marginal cost, and a reputation for reliability, discretion, and technical depth.

For collaborators, clients, and investors alike, the defining characteristic is not speed alone, but direction—a clear sense of where the systems are heading, and why they are being built the way they are.
